City 72

City 72 is an innovative, retro-futuristic educational game that addresses the challenge of engaging users in educational content. Set against the vibrant backdrop of a cyberpunk city, City 72 captivates players with its interactive cityscape. The game is designed to enhance GCSE knowledge through a blend of text-based decision-making, interactive challenges, and a dynamic storyline. This unique approach not only educates but also entertains, ensuring a captivating learning experience. As the project manager and lead designer of City 72, I played a pivotal role in bringing this vision to life. My responsibilities included overseeing the project's progression, coordinating with team members, ensuring design alignment with educational objectives, preparing and delivering a presentation using Canva, and conducting a comprehensive demo of the project for the team. By employing agile methodologies and practices, I successfully led the team to deliver this project within the ambitious two-week timeframe.

NC-News

NC News is a React application that I developed independently during week 9 of the Northcoders' software engineering bootcamp. This project serves as a demonstration of my expertise in diverse React concepts, including React DOM manipulation, routing, optimistic rendering, and error handling. The application seamlessly interacts with a custom-built back-end RESTful API that I created earlier in week six of the bootcamp. a heads-up: when you click on the link for the first time, it might take a little while to load. So, hang in there and stay patient! 😀 Currently, the app offers a range of functionalities, such as viewing a list of articles, accessing individual articles for detailed reading, and allowing users to vote on articles (with the restriction of only one vote per article). However, I am continuously working on expanding the feature set and will soon be incorporating additional functionalities to enhance the overall user experience.
